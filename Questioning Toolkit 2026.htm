Questioning Toolkit Report

 

1. Overview of Toolkit

1.1 Purpose

The questioning toolkit provides:

·         Structured strategies to improve classroom questioning

·         Techniques to increase participation and inclusivity

·         Methods to deepen thinking and improve response quality

 

1.2 Core Coverage

The toolkit includes:

·         Step-by-step processes for key questioning techniques

·         Strategy guidance (Why / When / What to consider)

·         Differentiation approaches

·         Inclusion strategies

·         Question design frameworks (e.g. Bloom’s Taxonomy)

 

2. Core Questioning Techniques

 

2.1 Think–Pair–Share

Process

1.      Establish partners

2.      Set the question with a goal and timeframe

3.      Build in thinking time

4.      Circulate and listen

5.      Use cold call to sample responses

Why use this

·         Promotes participation, confidence, and deeper thinking

·         Encourages low-stakes discussion

·         Supports quieter students

When to use

·         Checking understanding

·         Developing and refining answers

·         Exploring alternative approaches

What to consider

·         Keep questions focused

·         Use feedback from discussions

·         Build towards whole-class sharing

 

2.2 Show Me Boards

Process

1.      Give each student a board

2.      Set the question

3.      Build thinking time

4.      Signal response (e.g. 3-2-1 show)

5.      Sample and follow up

Why use this

·         Enables simultaneous responses from all students

·         Identifies misconceptions in real time

·         Supports inclusive participation

When to use

·         Throughout lessons (modelling, practice, retrieval, review)

·         Whole-class checks

What to consider

·         Use varied response types:

o   True/false

o   Confidence scales

o   Diagrams or annotations

·         Use as a whole-class assessment tool

 

2.3 Pose, Pause, Pounce, Bounce

Process

1.      Pose question

2.      Pause for thinking

3.      Pounce on a student

4.      Bounce question to others

5.      Build extended discussion

Why use this

·         Builds thinking time

·         Encourages wider participation

·         Promotes deeper thinking

When to use

·         Recall and retrieval

·         Developing explanations

·         Refining responses

What to consider

·         Plan pause time deliberately

·         Use follow-up responses to extend thinking

 

2.4 Say It Again Better

Process

1.      Ask question

2.      Acknowledge response

3.      Provide feedback

4.      Ask student to improve response

5.      Respond to improved answer

Why use this

·         Develops extended, high-quality responses

·         Builds vocabulary and precision

When to use

·         Developing subject terminology

·         Improving academic answers

What to consider

·         Use sentence stems and prompts

·         Apply no opt out routines

·         Maintain challenge while supporting participation

 

2.5 Probing Questions

Process

1.      Ask question

2.      Follow up with probing

3.      Listen and extend

4.      Involve other students

5.      Check broader understanding

Why use this

·         Promotes higher-order thinking

·         Deepens responses

When to use

·         Beyond recall

·         During analysis and evaluation

What to consider

·         Use prompts:

o   Why?

o   How?

o   Can you extend?

·         Link responses across students

 

2.6 Process Questions

Process

1.      Model thinking

2.      Emphasise how and why

3.      Ask students to explain reasoning

4.      Explore alternatives

Why use this

·         Develops understanding of methods

·         Builds problem-solving skills

When to use

·         After core knowledge is secure

·         During skill development

What to consider

·         Break processes into steps

·         Model thinking clearly

 

2.7 No Opt Out Questioning

Process

1.      Pose question

2.      Direct to student

3.      Provide support or bounce

4.      Return to original student

Why use this

·         Ensures participation from all students

·         Promotes accountability

When to use

·         Whole-class questioning

·         Recall and understanding checks

What to consider

·         Keep supportive and low-stakes

·         Use scaffolds to help success

 

2.8 Cold Calling

Process

1.      Ask whole-class question

2.      Provide thinking time

3.      Select student

4.      Respond and build

Why use this

·         Increases engagement

·         Broadens participation

When to use

·         Checking prior knowledge

·         Recapping learning

What to consider

·         Maintain positive tone

·         Combine with think time

 

2.9 Check for Understanding

Process

·         Ask question

·         Probe responses

·         Explore misconceptions

·         Decide to move on or reteach

Why use this

·         Identifies learning gaps

·         Supports adaptive teaching

When to use

·         End of lesson or topic

·         After key learning

What to consider

·         Use multiple techniques

·         Be prepared to reteach

 

2.10 Hinge Questions

Process

1.      Plan key question

2.      Ask all students

3.      Collect responses simultaneously

4.      Adapt teaching

Why use this

·         Provides quick whole-class check

·         Identifies misconceptions

When to use

·         Mid-lesson transition points

·         Before new content

What to consider

·         Ensure all students respond

·         Plan carefully in advance

 

2.11 Bonus Point Questions

Why use this

·         Encourages wider participation

·         Adds pace and energy

When to use

·         To extend responses

·         As part of mixed questioning strategies

What to consider

·         Avoid competitive exclusion

·         Maintain inclusivity

 

2.12 Randomised Questioning

Why use this

·         Ensures fair participation

·         Keeps all students engaged

When to use

·         Whole-class questioning

·         Alongside other strategies

What to consider

·         Keep low-stakes

·         Provide thinking time

 

3. Inclusive Questioning Strategies

 

3.1 Scaffolded Questions

·         Build from simple to complex

·         Break down tasks

·         Support all learners

Example progression:

·         What is happening?

·         Why is this happening?

·         How might this change?

 

3.2 Tiered Questions

·         Range from basic to higher-order

·         Support mixed-ability classes

·         Use Bloom’s Taxonomy

 

3.3 Choice of Response

Students can respond:

·         Verbally

·         Written

·         Visually

·         Digitally

Impact:

·         Supports diverse learners

·         Increases engagement

·         Promotes equity

 

3.4 Visual Questioning and Dual Coding

·         Combine images and text

·         Support recall and understanding

·         Improve access for complex ideas

 

3.5 Wait Time / Think Time

·         Improves response quality

·         Supports processing

·         Increases participation

 

3.6 Grouping

·         Supports differentiation

·         Promotes collaboration

·         Enables targeted questioning

 

 

4. Question Design and Depth

 

4.1 Bloom’s Taxonomy

Used to:

·         Structure progression

·         Develop deeper thinking

Levels include:

·         Recall

·         Understanding

·         Application

·         Analysis

·         Evaluation

 

4.2 Varying Question Types

Use a mix of:

·         Closed questions

·         Open questions

·         Probing questions

·         Reflective questions

 

4.3 Extension Questions

·         Build on initial responses

·         Promote deeper thinking

Example:

·         “What if…?”

·         “How would this change…?”

6.1 Lesson Start

·         Check prior knowledge

·         Use:

o   True/false

o   Quick recall

o   Hinge questions

 

6.2 During Learning

·         Develop understanding

·         Use:

o   Think–Pair–Share

o   Pose, Pause, Pounce, Bounce

o   Probing questions

o   Bloom’s questioning

 

6.3 End of Lesson (Plenary)

·         Check understanding and retrieval

·         Use:

o   Question games

o   Higher-order questions

o   Say it again better

o   Bonus questions

o   Return to previous responses

 

7. Key Principles Across the Toolkit

·         All students should think, not just volunteers

·         Build in structured thinking time

·         Use scaffolding to support and challenge

·         Promote inclusive participation

·         Adapt teaching based on responses

·         Move from simple to deeper thinking

·         Combine multiple questioning strategies
